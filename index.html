Davide Allegro

Ph.D. Student in Computer Vision and Robotics

Hello, I am Davide Allegro.

I am a Control System Engineer and I am currently pursuing the Ph.D. in Computer Vision and Robotics at the Intelligent Autonomous Systems Laboratory (IASLab), in the Department of Information Engineering (DEI) of the University of Padua.

I am deeply fascinated by robotics and artificial intelligence (AI), especially in their potential combination to tackle real-world challenges. My past and current experience, have provided me with valuable insights into the use of AI and Computer Vision in robotics.This has been instrumental in enabling the extraction of crucial information from our environment, thereby facilitating the development of intelligent robots designed for seamless interaction with both humans and their surroundings.

My ambitions extend well beyond simply pushing the boundaries of academic and technical achievements. I am driven by a vision to foster a future where humans and robots can coexist harmoniously and collaborate effectively towards common objectives, prioritising human safety. Research

I am a second year Ph.D. student in Computer Vision and Robotics at the University of Padova (Italy), under the supervision of Prof. Stefano Ghidoni. I am interested in combining computer vision and deep learning algorithms to tackle real-world challenges, especially in the field of robotics. My research is centered on single-camera and multi-camera hand-eye calibration, along with the 6D object pose estimation. I am also involved in enhancing multi-view geometry techniques for pose estimation, particularly within the dynamic framework of human-robot collaboration. Furthermore, I have a profound interest in innovating and applying deep learning architectures specifically designed for human action recognition. I am deeply intrigued by the study of foundation models and their possible application in real-world scenarios.
